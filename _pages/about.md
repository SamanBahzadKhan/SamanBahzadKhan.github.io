I am a Computer Science undergraduate at [LUMS](https://lums.edu.pk), working toward a PhD application in December 2026. Driven by a deep enthusiasm for research, I am broadly curious across many areas of computer science — from systems and distributed computing to machine learning and AI alignment.

My current work sits at the intersection of **LLM-based systems**, **preference optimization**, and **AI-driven automation**. I collaborate with [Dr. Ali Anwar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=) at the University of Minnesota, under whose mentorship I have developed two research projects currently under review at top venues:

- **LADs: Leveraging LLMs for AI-Driven DevOps** *(First Author, ICDCS '26)* — an agentic LLM framework that automates the full configuration-to-deployment pipeline through a self-refining feedback loop, improving deployment success from ≤22% to 68% across distributed systems.

- **Sem-DPO: Mitigating Semantic Inconsistency in Preference Optimization for Prompt Engineering** *(ACL ARR)* — a variant of DPO that preserves semantic consistency during prompt optimization for text-to-image generation, achieving 8–12% higher CLIP similarity over standard DPO.

Outside of research, I have gained industry experience as a Summer Intern at **Systems Limited**. I also have extensive experience reviewing papers for various **IEEE and ACM conferences**.
